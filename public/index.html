Welcome to the Smart Garden Project

Welcome to the Smart Garden Project, a pioneering initiative that merges advanced technology with the nurturing of nature.

This document serves as a guide to the structure and expectations of our development team as we embark on creating a fully automated smart garden within our campus greenhouse.

The Pride PlantLab is an automated sustainable community garden that will be located in the green house on the top of Thelma Ryan Hall.

This will be a place for undergraduate students to come and learn about data analytics, sustainable/smart agriculture, and technology.

Students from surrounding high schools and middle schools will be invited to participate in the community garden and learn about IoT in sustainable agriculture.

    The end goal of this project is threefold:

  1. To establish a fully functioning and sustainable food-growing operation in the greenhouse at the top of Thelma Ryan Hall, monitored and maintained by computer science, biology, and sociology students with interests in food or environmental sociology.

  2. To incorporate technology that utilizes smart IoT systems powered with data analytics capabilities and machine learning.

  3. to invite schools from the Hammond area and around the state to visit the project, sparking interest in pursuing degrees in biology, computer science, and sociology at southeastern.
